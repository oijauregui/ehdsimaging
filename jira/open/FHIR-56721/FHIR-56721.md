# FHIR-56721: Composition need not be signed by a single radiologist; allow Organization as attester.party; add guidance for legal authenticator and result validator

## Description

the document bundle represents the legally signable report and it includes all the data defining the report

This should clarify who is signing (if anyone) and at what point in time. DiagnosticReport.author already represents the user who is clinically (and in many cases legally) responsible. Some content in the ImagingReport bundle could change out from underneath a radiologist after they have signed, and we should not force radiologists to either re-sign or wait to sign until all information is present.

For example:


	Instance availability notifications may file after a radiologist has finished their result, or a technologist may finish documentation and annotation of a waveform.
	Communications may also change if other members of the radiology department are following up on critical results.
	Recommendations may be modified or documented discretely by later members of the care team, such as a mammography coordinator.
	If the History section references Conditions, those may be updated over time, and it shouldn't be expected that the EHR persist all versions of those conditions (note — it's also not expected that contained versions be used).



Beyond that, the radiologist should not be required to attest to the whole bundle, since it includes information (image availability, downstream Communications, Recommendations added by others) that the radiologist isn't responsible for. Org-level attestation is the right primitive.

Separately: please provide more guidance / examples around who the legal authenticator and result validator would be. Legal Authenticator is a SHALL; customers have struggled with this in the past (enough to have an FAQ). And attester:legalAuthenticator.party is currently 0..1 — legal attestation with no identified signer is not coherent.

Proposed resolution:

Add text making clear that the Composition may not actually be signed by a single person and that it may be generated on the fly in response to a request. This is reflected in the fact that attester is 0..* in the Composition profile (where only Practitioner and PractitionerRole may be used as .party). In addition to use for Signatures, the Composition can be used to communicate an ordering of elements. Allow Organization as a valid attester.party, as is allowed in the EU Core Composition profile, so that org-level attestation is supported.

Add guidance and at least one example each for the legal authenticator and result validator.

Raise attester:legalAuthenticator.party to 1..1.
